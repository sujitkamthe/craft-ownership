Craft & Ownership

A Field Guide for Senior Engineers

For engineers who take a feature end-to-end — and raise the craft bar along the way.

Two skills that turn a strong developer into the engineer a team can’t do without.

Explore the Guide

Who This Is For

This guide is for engineers, roughly 4–8 years in, who already write solid code and want to grow into owning features end-to-end — and lifting the craft bar of the team around them. If you close a ticket and find yourself asking “but did the feature actually land well?”, this is written for you.

It’s not a path to management. If you want to lead people, build teams, and run hiring, see the Beyond IC companion guide — that’s a different road, and this one stops before it.

And it’s not a persuasion pitch either. It assumes you want more ownership and need the how. If you’re still deciding whether ownership is for you, read Foundations first and see if it lands.

How to Use It

It’s a field guide, not a course. Don’t read it front-to-back. Pick the area that matches what’s on your plate this month, skim the frameworks, try one thing this week, come back when the situation shifts.

There are no grades and no scorecards. The self-assessment is there to help you spot your smallest next step, not to rank you against anyone — come back to it every few months.

Small, consistent steps are what turn a strong coder into the engineer a team relies on. One framework, applied well, beats ten read and forgotten.
