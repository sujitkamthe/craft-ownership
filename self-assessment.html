Self-Assessment

An honest checklist to see where you are today. No grades, no scores reported anywhere, no one watching. Pick this up every few months and watch yourself grow.

Check what’s true for you today — not what you intend to do, not what you did once last year. Your ticks are saved in your browser so you can come back to this page over time. Nothing is sent anywhere.

When you’re done, the summary at the bottom will point you at the sections of the guide most worth your next hour.

Reading Your Score

There are 54 items across 11 areas. Treat the number as a map, not a verdict. Nobody ticks all of them — including engineers you admire. The goal is to know where you are and pick one thing to work on next.

🏆

45–54: You’re the engineer teams fight to keep. Your next growth is probably in scope — more ambitious initiatives, wider influence, or preparing for the step beyond this guide. See the Beyond IC companion guide when you’re ready for people leadership.

📈

32–44: Solid. You’re already operating as a senior engineer across most areas. Pick the three items you didn’t tick that feel most relevant, and work on one each month. The “influence” and “delivery” sections are usually where the next unlock lives.

⚙️

18–31: You’re a capable IC, growing toward senior. The next step is less about coding skill and more about ownership, voice, and the scan-propose-drive loop. Start with Foundations and Code Reviews; pick one page a week.

💡

0–17: This guide may be one step ahead of where you are right now — and that’s fine. The earlier Curiosity & Commitment guide covers the habits this one assumes (finishing what you start, propose-don’t-complain, speaking up, basic craft). Build those first; then come back. If you want to start here anyway, read Foundations this week, pick one behaviour to change, and re-take this in a month.
